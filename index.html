Interests

What am I interested in?

Robotics is something I’ve always been fascinated by, and this can be seen by the research and the projects I’ve done and am currently working on. I believe robotics is a fast-growing field in the tech industry, next to AI. The immense growth of AI in the past few years has played a crucial role in the rise of robotics, as the two fields are intertwined. Many people think robotics only deals with the development of human, dog, or bird-like structured robots, but that's not the case. Robotics plays crucial roles in healthcare, manufacturing industries, agriculture, space, and deep-sea exploration, and more. For instance, let’s look at self-driving cars in detail. Without the principles and applications of robotics, it would not be possible to produce these self-driving cars. Key Robotics principles like Perception, Path Planning, Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) and Motion Control play a crucial role in enabling the vehicle to map and understand its environment and make real-time decisions for efficient and safe autonomous driving.

My ongoing research on the development of Mobile Waste Collection Robot has led me to delve deeper into the field of Robotics and explore the complex robotic movement algorithms, sensor and camera integration, robotic arm design and development, programming software for low level control of mechanical parts, and most importantly integrating AI to improve the robot's decision-making capabilities, object recognition, and efficient waste sorting and collection processes. Additionally, the Hexapod Robot project, where I’m building a six legged, spider like robot, has been important in deepening my understanding of the field of Robotics.

As they say, "AI is the brain, robotics is the body," I had to explore and learn about AI while working on my robotics research and projects. Specifically, deep learning and computer vision play significant roles in my robotics projects. For instance, in my research on the development of a Mobile Waste Collection Robot, I am using deep learning and computer vision libraries like TensorFlow and OpenCV to enable autonomous navigation, trash detection, and classification for the robot I’m building. We can also discuss my IntelliDrone System project, where I used Python's computer vision library, OpenCV, to develop multifunctional software for a DJI Tello quadcopter drone. The software I built includes features such as surveillance, facial recognition, and tracking.

Working on the hardware parts of robots requires knowledge and skills in programming microcontrollers and microprocessors, which are generally part of embedded systems. I’m using microcontrollers like Arduino in my Hexapod Robot project, where it control the movement and coordination of the robot's six legs to enable precise and synchronized locomotion. I’m also working with microprocessors like the Raspberry Pi in my research on the development of a Mobile Waste Collection Robot, where it handles the complex processing tasks for autonomous navigation, sensor data analysis, and AI-based waste detection and classification. Additionally, we can also look at my Smart Garage Door Control system, where I used a Raspberry Pi microprocessor, Relay Module, and Magnetic Switches to enable remote monitoring and control of my home’s garage door with commands and updates managed through Firebase connected to Flutter Mobile App.

Lastly, mobile application development is another field I'm interested in, next to robotics, deep learning, computer vision, and embedded systems. To be specific, I use Flutter framework and Dart programming language to develop mobile applications for Android and iOS devices. For instance, I built an Uber-Eats Clone app, a replica of the popular Uber Eats app, designed to mimic its key functionalities. Additionally, I developed Speech-Craft, a Spanish language learning app, and Matchtoria, a card-matching game. I can also bring up the app I built for the Smart Garage Door Control system mentioned above.
